                    <h3 class="text-2xl font-semibold mb-4 text-blue-700">Key Partnership Frameworks</h3>

                    <p class="mb-4">

                        The AI support strategies outlined on this site are derived from the principles of these foundational frameworks for family, school, and community partnerships:

                    </p>

                    <ul class="resource-list list-disc pl-5 space-y-3">

                        <li>

                            <strong>Dual Capacity-Building Framework for Family-School Partnerships:</strong> Emphasizes building capacity in both school staff and families for effective engagement, focusing on creating optimal "opportunity conditions" and achieving shared outcomes for student success.

                        </li>

                        <li>

                            <strong>Epstein Partnership Model:</strong> Identifies six key types of involvement (parenting, communicating, volunteering, learning at home, decision-making, collaborating with the community) as overlapping spheres of influence, promoting inclusive practices.

                        </li>

                        <li>

                            <strong>Equitable Collaboration Framework:</strong> Advocates for equitable collaboration with families and community members, especially underserved populations, through "collective impact" efforts that build authentic relationships and strengthen individual and collective capacity across sectors.

                        </li>

                        <li>

                            <strong>Inclusive Partnership Framework:</strong> Highlights effective and inclusive communication as a core dimension, stressing personal, warm, frequent, culturally appropriate, and non-judgmental interactions to build strong relationships between school leaders, teachers, and families.

                        </li>

                        <li>

                            <strong>NAFSCE Educator Preparation Framework for Family and Community Partnerships:</strong> Outlines core competencies for educators (Reflect, Connect, Collaborate, Co-Lead) in family and community engagement, recognizing its foundational role in student achievement, school improvement, and family wellbeing.

                        </li>

                        <li>

                            <strong>PTA National Standards for Family-School Partnerships:</strong> Provides concrete guideposts for how schools and parent organizations should work together, focusing on welcoming all families, effective communication, community collaboration, supporting student success, sharing power, and advocating for every child.

                        </li>

                    </ul>
